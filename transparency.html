Transparency — Catalogue update commitment

App: Swiss Citizenship Test · Developer: Vincenzo Casini · Last updated: 10 May 2026

Why this page exists

This app sources its question pools from official cantonal websites for 7 Swiss cantons. Because each canton publishes its catalogue differently and updates it on its own schedule, users and institutions need a way to verify how this app keeps its data in sync with official sources. This page documents the verification process, the declared service level, the reporting channel for discrepancies, and the public audit trail.

This commitment is voluntary. The app is not affiliated with any government authority. It is independently developed by a naturalization applicant.

Service Level — what we promise

Source verification: all 7 official cantonal sources are checked once every 24 hours via an automated pipeline.

Standard update window: when a change is detected, the updated catalogue is shipped to users in a new app version within 14 days.

Emergency window: critical issues (factual errors flagged by users or canton authorities) are addressed within 72 hours.

Audit trail: every check is logged in a public audit log (see below).

Public audit log

Every source verification — successful or failed — is recorded in a structured audit log. The log includes timestamp, source URL, response status, content hash, and detected changes. The log is published as part of the public scraping repository.

Versioning policy

Each catalogue snapshot is identified by:

  • An ISO date (when the snapshot was captured from the official source)
  • A content hash (SHA-256 of the normalized catalogue)
  • An app version (the first published app release that ships this snapshot)

Historical snapshots are preserved in the public scraping repository so that any past version can be inspected and compared against the live one.

About this app

Swiss Citizenship Test is a free, ad-free, account-free study tool for the Swiss naturalization knowledge test. It is independently developed by a naturalization applicant living in Switzerland. It is not affiliated with any government authority. Its purpose is to complement official preparation tools — not to replace them.

For questions about this transparency commitment or to discuss institutional collaboration: swisscitizenshiptest@gmail.com.
