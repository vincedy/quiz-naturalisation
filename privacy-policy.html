Privacy Policy

App: Swiss Naturalization Test · Developer: Vincenzo Casini · Last updated: 15 July 2026

Summary

This app stores quiz progress on your device and synchronizes pseudonymous data (no personal information) with our cloud service to enable the leaderboard and cross-device features. Unless you choose to sign in (Google or Apple), we do not collect names, emails, contacts, location, or any personally identifying information. If you sign in, we collect your email address to manage your account — see “Optional Sign-In” below.

What We Collect

The app creates an anonymous account on our servers (Supabase) automatically when first launched. The following pseudonymous data is stored:

  • An anonymous user ID (random UUID, not linked to your identity)
  • A device identifier (random UUID generated on this device). On iOS, this corresponds to Apple's Identifier for Vendor (IDFV), a vendor-scoped, non-advertising identifier.
  • Your selected canton and commune (e.g., "Vaud / Morges")
  • Your selected app language
  • An auto-generated display name (e.g., "Donnola Leale") used for the leaderboard
  • Your quiz progress: exam results, game scores, answered questions, mastery progress
  • The app version you are using

Unless you sign in, we do not collect: your name, email, phone number, address, contacts, photos, location (GPS); no IDFA (Apple advertising identifier), nor any device identifier that could be used to track you across apps.

Optional Sign-In (Google / Apple)

By default the app runs on an anonymous account and we do not know who you are. You can optionally sign in with Google or Apple to secure your account and sync your progress across devices. If you sign in, we collect your email address (with Sign in with Apple you may use Apple's Private Relay address) and link it to your account. We use it only to identify your account and enable cross-device sync — never for advertising — and we never sell or share it. You can use the app fully without ever signing in.

Why We Collect It

This data is used solely to: enable the leaderboard, allow you to compare your progress with other users, and synchronize your progress across devices. We do not sell, share, or use this data for advertising or any other purpose.

When you play in competitive mode, your display name, your selected commune and your score are visible to other users on public leaderboards.

Legal Basis

We process your data on the following legal bases (Art. 6(1) GDPR; the Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection, nFADP, provides equivalent bases):

  • Anonymous account and progress sync — Art. 6(1)(b), performance of a contract.
  • Selected canton and commune — Art. 6(1)(b), performance of a contract.
  • Public leaderboard (display name, canton, commune, score) — Art. 6(1)(a), your consent.
  • Email from optional Google/Apple sign-in — Art. 6(1)(b), performance of a contract.
  • Technical and crash data — Art. 6(1)(f), our legitimate interest in keeping the app working.

Local Storage

A copy of your progress is also kept locally on your device for offline use.

Third-Party Services

The app uses Supabase (supabase.com) as a backend service to store the data described above. Supabase Inc. is based in the United States; your data is stored within the European Union (AWS Ireland region, eu-west-1). Supabase support staff in the United States may access the data, which constitutes a transfer to a third country. This transfer is covered by Standard Contractual Clauses (SCC) together with the Swiss addendum recognised by the FDPIC, under Supabase's own privacy terms (supabase.com/privacy). The app uses no advertising networks, analytics tools, or tracking technologies.

The app may show an optional donation link that opens PayPal in your browser. We receive no payment data; any transaction is handled entirely by PayPal under its own privacy terms. On iOS, optional donations use (or will soon use) Apple In-App Purchase instead of an external link.

For our update commitment and source verification process, see our Transparency page.

Technical Data Collection

We collect technical device information (device model, operating system version, app version, platform identifier) for crash diagnosis and performance monitoring, via our own backend (Supabase, self-managed). This data is not personally identifiable and is processed exclusively to keep the app working. We use no third-party analytics, crash-reporting or telemetry SDKs (no Sentry, no Firebase, no Crashlytics, no Google Analytics).

Data Retention

We keep your data only as long as needed:

  • Account and progress: deleted automatically after 24 months of inactivity.
  • Leaderboard entry: kept until you delete your account.
  • Crash and technical logs: 90 days maximum.
  • Deletion requests sent by email: kept for 12 months as proof the request was handled.

Data Deletion

You can delete your account and data at any time, in two ways:

  • Directly in the app (immediate): Settings → Account → "Delete account and data".
  • By email: write to swisscitizenshiptest@gmail.com and we will process the deletion within 30 days.

Deletion removes your display name, avatar, email and linked social identities, and soft-deletes your account identifier. Anonymous game data is retained for the integrity of historical leaderboards. Local data on your device can be deleted by uninstalling the app. For full details on what is deleted and how, see our Account & Data Deletion page.

Your Rights

Under the GDPR (and the Swiss nFADP) you have the right to access your data (Art. 15), rectify it (Art. 16), erase it (Art. 17), restrict its processing (Art. 18), receive it in a portable format (Art. 20), object to processing (Art. 21), and withdraw your consent at any time (Art. 7(3)). To exercise any of these, email swisscitizenshiptest@gmail.com; we reply within 30 days. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority (see the EDPB list of national authorities): in Italy, the Garante per la protezione dei dati personali; in Switzerland, the Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner (edoeb.admin.ch).

Data Breach Notifications

If a data breach is likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify the competent supervisory authority within 72 hours (Art. 33 GDPR). If the breach is likely to result in a high risk, we will also inform affected users directly (Art. 34 GDPR).

Children's Privacy

This app is intended for users aged 16 and over. We do not knowingly collect data from children under 16. Some EU member states set a lower digital-consent age (between 13 and 16; for example 14 in Italy); in those countries the national age applies, but never below 13. Below that age, the app may be used only with the consent of a parent or guardian. If you believe a child has used the app without such consent, contact us to request deletion.

Changes to This Policy

If this policy is updated, the revised version will be published at this URL with a new "Last updated" date.

Questi dati servono esclusivamente per: abilitare la classifica, permetterti di confrontare i tuoi progressi con altri utenti, sincronizzare i tuoi progressi tra dispositivi. Non vendiamo, condividiamo né usiamo questi dati per pubblicità o altri scopi.

Base giuridica

Trattiamo i tuoi dati sulle seguenti basi giuridiche (art. 6(1) GDPR; la legge federale svizzera sulla protezione dei dati, nLPD, prevede basi equivalenti):

  • Account anonimo e sincronizzazione dei progressi — art. 6(1)(b), esecuzione di un contratto.
  • Cantone e comune selezionati — art. 6(1)(b), esecuzione di un contratto.
  • Classifica pubblica (nome visualizzato, cantone, comune, punteggio) — art. 6(1)(a), il tuo consenso.
  • E-mail dall'accesso facoltativo Google/Apple — art. 6(1)(b), esecuzione di un contratto.
  • Dati tecnici e di crash — art. 6(1)(f), il nostro legittimo interesse a far funzionare l'app.

Memorizzazione locale

Una copia dei tuoi progressi viene mantenuta anche localmente sul tuo dispositivo per l'uso offline.

Servizi di terze parti

L'app usa Supabase (supabase.com) come servizio backend per memorizzare i dati descritti sopra. Supabase Inc. ha sede negli Stati Uniti; i tuoi dati sono archiviati all'interno dell'Unione Europea (regione AWS Irlanda, eu-west-1). Il personale di supporto di Supabase negli Stati Uniti può accedere ai dati, il che costituisce un trasferimento verso un paese terzo. Questo trasferimento è coperto da Clausole Contrattuali Standard (SCC) e dall'addendum svizzero riconosciuto dall'IFPDT, secondo le condizioni sulla privacy di Supabase (supabase.com/privacy). L'app non usa reti pubblicitarie, strumenti di analisi né tecnologie di tracciamento.

L'app può mostrare un link di donazione facoltativo che apre PayPal nel browser. Per il nostro impegno di aggiornamento e il processo di verifica delle fonti, consulta la nostra pagina di Trasparenza.

Raccogliamo informazioni tecniche sul dispositivo (modello, versione del sistema operativo, versione dell'app, identificatore della piattaforma) per la diagnosi dei crash e il monitoraggio delle prestazioni tramite il nostro backend (Supabase, self-managed). Questi dati non sono identificabili personalmente e sono trattati esclusivamente per far funzionare l'app. Non usiamo SDK di terze parti per analisi, crash reporting o telemetria (niente Sentry, niente Firebase, niente Crashlytics, niente Google Analytics).

Conservazione dei dati

Conserviamo i tuoi dati solo per il tempo necessario:

  • Voce in classifica: conservata fino alla cancellazione del tuo account.

Eliminazione dei dati

Puoi eliminare il tuo account e i tuoi dati in qualsiasi momento, in due modi:

  • Direttamente nell'app (immediato): Impostazioni → Account → « Elimina account e dati ».
  • Via e-mail: scrivi a swisscitizenshiptest@gmail.com e tratteremo l'eliminazione entro 30 giorni.

L'eliminazione rimuove il tuo nome visualizzato, l'avatar, l'e-mail e le identità social collegate, e disattiva (soft-delete) l'identificativo del tuo account. I dati di gioco anonimi vengono conservati per l'integrità delle classifiche storiche. I dati locali sul tuo dispositivo possono essere eliminati disinstallando l'app. Per tutti i dettagli su cosa viene eliminato e come, consulta la nostra pagina Cancellazione account e dati.

Privacy dei minori

Questa app è destinata alle persone di età pari o superiore a 16 anni. Non raccogliamo consapevolmente dati da minori di 16 anni. Alcuni Stati membri dell'UE fissano un'età di consenso digitale più bassa (tra 13 e 16 anni; ad esempio 14 in Italia); in quei paesi si applica l'età nazionale, ma mai sotto i 13 anni. Al di sotto di tale età, l'app può essere usata solo con il consenso di un genitore o tutore. Se ritieni che un minore abbia usato l'app senza tale consenso, contattaci per richiedere l'eliminazione.
